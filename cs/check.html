  • Elocution Competition

    Elocution Competition

    Elocution Competition

    Description:

    The art of impactful speech and communication is invaluable in both personal and professional spheres. As budding engineers, developing eloquence to articulate ideas persuasively will be a distinguishing capability as we build our careers. This competition serves as a platform for participants to exhibit their oratory prowess. The objective is to engage in an exchange of ideas, insights, and diverse perspectives through the tool of effective speech.

    Rules & Guidelines

    1. All UG and Diploma students of Chemical Engineering and Allied Courses are eligible.
    2. Each participant will have a maximum of 10 minutes for their elocution. Exceeding this risks disqualification.
    3. A warning bell will ring at 8 minutes, and the final bell at 10 minutes. Participants must finish their speech by the final bell.
    4. Judges will assess participants based on parameters like content, language, delivery, voice modulation, body language and overall impact. Marks may be deducted for exceeding the time limit.
    5. Our judging panel has been carefully selected for their expertise. They will evaluate speeches fairly based on the assessment criteria.
    6. Participants should focus on articulating their thoughts clearly while engaging the audience. The purpose is to inform, inspire and connect through the powerful medium of eloquent speech.
    7. We request the audience to kindly refrain from disturbances during speeches. Applause is encouraged at the conclusion of each elocution to appreciate the participant.

    Group Discussion

    Description:

    The group discussion technique is a very effective way to judge the candidateâ€™s ability to work in a group. Engage in dynamic discussions that foster diverse perspectives and collaborative thinking. Our group discussions provide a platform for exchanging ideas, promoting active participation, and cultivating a rich environment for collective learning. Join us to amplify your voice and contribute to the vibrant exchange of thoughts and insights.

    Do's of participating in a GD

    1. All UG and Diploma students of Chemical Engineering and Allied Courses are eligible.
    2. Be gentle and sure in your presentation of views.
    3. Speak to the point and make sure that you do not repeat the points.
    4. Be calm and composed while speaking.
    5. Listening to others is also an important aspect of participation in the group discussion, so listen to others.
    6. Have a respectful attitude towards the viewpoints of others.

    Don'ts of participating in a GD

    1. Do not go overboard in exhibiting your knowledge even if you know the topic well.
    2. Do not interrupt other members when they are speaking.
    3. Do not change your opinion about the topic just because most of the other participants are having an opinion different from yours.
    4. Do not ask irrelevant questions.
    5. Do not let your personal biases about the topic enter the discussion.
