  • Scintilla

    CSE(AI & ML)

    Scintilla: A Programming Contest

    Description:

    SCINTILLA Challenge is an exciting and intellectually stimulating technical quiz program that aims to test and enhance participants' knowledge across a wide range of technical subjects. From computer science and engineering to beyond, this quiz program covers a diverse array of topics, making it suitable for students, professionals, and tech enthusiasts alike. This event consists of two rounds named Prelims and Mains:

    • Prelims: It is a multiple-choice question round. It consists of 20 questions, and the participants need to complete it in 10 minutes. After this round, the first 10 teams will be selected and moved to the next round.
    • Mains: (Consists of 5 rounds)
    1. Round-1: Beginner’s Luck: This round is based on coding. Basic level questions are given to each team (can write in any language like C, C++, Java, Python). If the team can complete the questions in the given time, they will get 20 points. In this round, you can pass the question to your adjacent team if your team is unable to solve the question. If the adjacent team solves the passed questions in the allotted time, they can gain 10 more points.
      • Time allotted: 5 minutes
      • No. of Questions given: 2
      • Points if solved directly: 20 per question
      • Points if the question is passed: 10 points

    2. Round-2: Knowledge Flip Fiesta: This round is based on Aptitude. In this, participants are requested to pick a card from the stack. These cards consist of different topics in Aptitude (like Age, Time & Work, Percentage, Profit & Loss, Clocks, Time, Speed, Distance, Relations, etc.). The participants are given a Life line, and it can be used when the team wants to change their topic. The Life line can be used only once. The questions are not passed to other teams.
      • Time allotted: 4 minutes
      • No. of Questions given: 5
      • Points: 10 points per question

    3. Round-3: Reel Riddle: In this round, a 30 seconds video clip is displayed for each team. After the video clip, questions are asked based on the shown video. The same video is not played again.
      • Time allotted: 2 minutes
      • No. of Questions asked: 5
      • Points: 5 points per question

    4. Round-4: Algorithmic Enigma: In this round, all the teams are given pseudo codes. The teams are given different given different questions. In this, the participants are required to find the errors or fill the missing parts in the given code.
      • Time allotted: 4 minutes
      • No. of Questions given: 5
      • Points: 5 points per question

    5. Round-5: Fast and Furious: For this round, top 4 teams are selected. 1 minute per team is allotted. The maximum number of questions can be answered in a minute.
      • No. of Questions: Based on Time (1 Minute)
      • Points: 5 points per question

    RULES AND GUIDELINES:

    • All UG and PG students are eligible.
    • Only team entries are eligible.
    • A team shall consist of a maximum of two persons.
    • Replacement of any participant of a team is not allowed after registration.
    • The prelims round will be conducted in any one of the computer labs within the campus on the day of the event. The participants will be informed of the venue and time in due course of time.
    • Each team would have to answer 20 multiple choice objective type questions.
    • Time limit will be 10 minutes for the entire session.
    • Only 10 teams would be selected for the FINAL STAGE.
    • Institution student Identity Card is required.
    Join us for an intellectually stimulating and fun-filled SCINTILLA Challenge, where technology meets trivia! Test your technical prowess, learn from the best, and emerge as the TechMaster.

    Colloquy

    Description:

    Colloquy is a group discussion event to screen the candidate's personality traits and skills that it desires in its members. The group of candidates is given a topic or situation, given a few minutes time to think and then asked to discuss the topic among themselves for 15 to 20 minutes. Evaluation is done by the subject experts based on the performance.

    Rules

    1. Topics for discussion can be from a wide range of issues in society.
    2. Topics for discussion will be announced 5 minutes prior to the discussion.
    3. The discussion time will be pre-allotted by the judges, usually around 12-15 minutes.
    4. Participants are divided into groups, each group consisting of appropriate participants.
    5. Participants to the final round will be selected based on the marks awarded by the judge.
    6. The students who have qualified in Prelims are eligible to participate in Semi-Finals.
    7. The top performance of each Semi-Final group will be selected for Finals.

    Finals

    1. Topics for discussion can be from a wide range of issues in society.
    2. The discussion time will be pre-allotted by the judge.
    3. Winners in the final round will be solely selected by the panel of judges.
    4. The decision of the judges is final.

  • Invincible of SOIREE

    CSE

    Invincible of SOIREE

    Description:

    Discover the excitement of the “Invincible of SOIREE” challenge, a captivating technical quest designed to evaluate and enrich participant’s expertise across a board spectrum of technical subjects. This program enhances the knowledge of subjects encompassing computer science and engineering. Join us for the rewarding experience that goes beyond conventional boundaries and enhancing your knowledge in the dynamic world of technology.

    This program consists of the two stages:
    1. Prelims
    2. Mains (4 rounds)
    Prelims

    It is a multiple-choice questions round. It contains 20 questions varying from general reasoning to the basic level of programming. All the participants are supposed to complete the prelims within 10 min.

    No. of teams to be selected: 20

    Mains:
    Round -1: Sudo Hash chronicle

    Time allotted: 25 minutes

    This round is based on basic hash mapping and solving Sudoku. In this round, a hash function is given, and the key values are given in the puzzle (i.e. crossword), and the key value is processed through the hash function, and the hash values generated are used in solving the “Sudoku”.

    No. of groups to be selected: 10

    Round -2: Tech Charades

    Time allotted: 40 minutes

    In this round, one of the team members is given a word, and he has to act and describe the given word while the other teammate tries to guess the given word.

    No. of groups to be selected: 6

    Round -3: Code Blitz

    Time allotted: 100 minutes

    • This game starts with players solving a pool of questions for earning blitz points (BP).
    • Once a team has a certain amount of points, the team will have access to the main question pool.
    • Both members of the team can discuss and choose their main question (from the pool) and specify the question to volunteer.
    • Once a question is chosen, that team will not be able to change their question.
    • After choosing, the players can continue earning BP and now they can also start purchasing utilities using BP.
    • When a team is ready with enough utilities, they can start coding the main problem.
    • If a team wants a few more utilities than what they already purchased, they can purchase a few more at an increased price.
    • Price is increased only if they’ve started coding the main problem.
    • The fastest team to solve their selected question will be qualified.
    Round -4: Byte Talks

    Time allotted: 20 minutes

    In this round, top teams will be competing for the title ‘Invincible of SOIREE’. Each team will choose a random topic (Lucky dip). For each topic, there are a set of restricted keywords that shouldn’t be used in their speech. Each team will have a few minutes of time for preparation to deliver their speech.

    RULES AND GUIDELINES:
    • All UG and PG students are eligible.
    • Only team entries are eligible.
    • A team shall consist of a maximum of two persons.
    • Replacement of any participant of a team is not allowed after registration.
    • The prelims round will be conducted in any one of the computer labs within the campus on the day of the event (Will be intimated after coming to college campus). Mains will be conducted in Computer lab/Seminar hall.
    • Institution student Identity Card is required.
